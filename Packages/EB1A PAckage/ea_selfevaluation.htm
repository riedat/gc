Self-Evaluation Questions as a Guidance to
Increase Your EB1-Extraordinary Ability Petition Approval Chance

To decide if you qualify for the EB1 Extraordinary Ability immigration category, the following questions will help you determine and increase your chance of approval. Even though this is not an exact checklist of the qualification criteria, if you can answer five or six of the questions in an affirmative way, then you have a chance of making a case for EB1-EA petition. The exact outcome of your case will depend on how well you present it, among other things. 

To find out your chance of applying for U.S. permanent residence under the EB1-EA category, simply answer these questions and give a brief description of your work. Try to emphasize the significance of your work.

  • What is your research specialty? How many years of full-time research or teaching experience do you have?

  • Do you have an advanced degree (Masters or PhD)?  Can you argue that you are an established researcher or an expert in your field based on your professional experience? (list your academic degrees and area of your specialization, as well as the dates attended)

  • Do you have papers published in journals that are distributed nationally or internationally? How many citations of your published papers are in SCI, EI or other major science and engineering indexes? Have you wrote or published any academic book or book chapter in your field?

  • Are you working as a professor, researcher in a U.S. university, or doing research at an institution or a private company? What is the specific topic of your current research?

  • Do you hold a position which is critical for the operation of a company, and are you responsible for work of others? How critical is your work for the success of that company? 

  • Did you start a successful business in a technology industry? Was your role critical to the success of that company?

  • What is the potential application of your current research? What specific problem is addressed by your research? What do you hope to discover or achieve in your current research?

  • What have you discovered so far, or what progress have you made so far? Why is your work significant? What is the potential application of your discovery?

  • Do you have any evidence to prove that you are an excellent researcher with extraordinary ability? Have you developed or improved any research tools or methods? If so, what are they?
  • Do you have any professional membership? Are you affiliated with any academic societies or associations? Are you at a middle or senior level? Are you a member of an academic organization or society for which the membership criterion is not just by application, but by nomination or election?  
  • Have you received any request for reprints of your published papers? Do you have any proof or evidence to indicate the significance of your work? Have your research results been reported by the professional media or any other news media or agencies?

  • Have you ever won any academic prize, awards or honors that are above the average level? For example, have you ever won the national, state, or conference awards?

  • Do you have any patent or patent application? Do you have approved or pending patents? How many?  

  • Have you reviewed academic papers for professional journals? Have you been invited to judge, review or comment on other peer's research work? For example, have you acted as a referee in a professional conference?  

  • Is your salary above the average in the same position in your field? Have you commanded an above average salary in the past?  

  • Can you list 3-5 professors, academic experts or public officers who can write reference letters for you?

  • Does your work improve the health care, education, economy, housing, or environment of the United States?

  • Is your work requested by any government agency? (for example: NIH, VA, NSF, Army, Navy, NASA, DOE, DOT, DOD, etc.)

If some of the questions seem not applicable to your situation, just do the best you can to describe your work. Don't be shy or modest, you need to be aggressive to be successful in your petition. If you can answer some of the questions successfully, you may qualify for applying for U.S. permanent residence under the EB1-Extraordinary Ability category.
