How to Assemble Your Application Documents in a Nicely
Package for Immigration Officer to Easily Locate the Evidence

1. How to Assemble Your Application Materials

We believe how to assemble your materials may directly affect your application result. A nicely ordered application package should be easy for an immigration officer to locate the evidence. On USCIS website, they provide some general guidelines about how to assemble the application materials.

As you gather the documentary evidence to support your application, prepare an index of the documents you are submitting, and make a two whole punch at the top. Make an exact duplicate copy of whatever you are submitting. Put everything together with ACCO fasteners - the fastener with the two prongs that poke through and a bar with the little sliding clamps. Put the check of the application fee, application form, and the petition cover letter on top.

In preparing your application packet, please follow these guidelines:

1) Put your check of application fee at the top of your application packet. Thereafter, it should include your application cover letter, application form, reference letters, and supporting documents.

2) Mark both the envelope and the cover letter as to the nature of the submission. Example: ORIGINAL SUBMISSION, or RESPONSE TO REQUEST FOR ADDITIONAL INFORMATION - etc.

3) Use the appropriate mailing address and mark both the envelope and the cover letter as to the form type. Example: I-140; I-485; or I-129, etc.

4) Do not use binders or folders that cannot be easily disassembled.

5) Use ACCO fasteners to hold together thick or bulky applications or petitions. Two-hole punching the top of the material for easy placement in the file is appreciated.

6) The use of tabs assist in locating items listed as attachments. The tabs should be placed on the bottom and not the side for ease in filing.

7) Avoid using heavy-duty staples; instead use ACCO fasteners or heavy clips.

8) Avoid submitting originals unless specifically required. Avoid submitting oversized documentation when possible. 

9) Keep copies of all submissions. Don't assume the USCIS officer will have access to a prior file or record. Submit as complete a packet as possible so the case can be adjudicated from what you submit. Submit a complete packet of information for each petition. If officers have to review prior files or records, the adjudication of the case can be delayed substantially.

10) Be sure to complete all pertinent items on the petition or application. Ensure all entries on the forms are legible. Do note enter "N/A" when "None" is appropriate.

11) USCIS no longer routinely requires submission of original documents or "certified copies." Instead, ordinary legible photocopies of such documents will be acceptable for initial filing and approval of petitions and applications. At the discretion of the officer, original documents may still be required in individual cases. Please be advised that USCIS no longer returns  original documents submitted with the exception of Certificates of Naturalization, Forms I-551, Permanent Resident Card, Forms I-94,  Arrival/Departure Document, valid passports, or those specifically requested by the officer. Such documents will be returned when they are no longer needed.

2. More Tips of How to Assemble Your Application Materials

1) Divide your supporting documents into different categories and put related documents together, such as your publications, awards, honors, evidences of your impacts on your field. You must be creative with this issue.

2) Add one cover page in front of each category. It is better to use colored paper instead of white one for cover page to distinguish cover pages from the other documents. You can use colored paper for your cover pages. Provide short description about the document in the cover paper, such as publications list, evidences of my judge others work, emails inquiring my work, etc. This will ensure that the immigration office will not get messed up all your documents. 

3) Attach index tabs at the bottle of the cover pages of each category. In this way, an immigration officer can quickly find the document that he or she wants.

3. Example of Assembled Application Materials

Based on USCIS guidelines and the easy to locate principle, the application materials is assembled using following steps.

1) Put the sorted application materials in a pile.

2) Using a two-hole punch to punch two holes on the top. Make sure these holes are aligned.

3) Using Complete Fastener Sets to fasten application material into several pieces.

4) Write on index name on tabs and attached index tabs at the bottom of each cover pages.

5) Using rubber bands to fasten all your application together, including your check, application forms, and other supporting documents.

4.  How to Submit Certified Translations for Foreign Language Documents

You should submit certified translations for foreign language documents. The translator must certify that he or she is competent to translate and that  the translation is accurate. The certification format should include the certifier's name, signature, address, and date of certification. A suggested format is:

Certification by Translator

I [typed name], certify that I am fluent in the English and ________ languages, and that the above/attached document is an accurate 
translation of the document attached entitled ______________________________.

Signature_________________________________
