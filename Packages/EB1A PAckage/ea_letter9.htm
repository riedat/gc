Re: Dr. Kevin Yong's I-140 Immigrant Petition for an Alien of Extraordinary Ability

Dear USCIS Officer

It is my pleasure to support Dr. Kevin Yong's petition for immigration to the United States. I am writing to provide evidence that his research and development work is exceptional and he has Extraordinary Ability in research. 

Dr. Yong is presently a researcher at General Electric Research & Development Center (GE R&D Center), developing the Electronic Document Management System (EDMS). Dr. Yong is an excellent researcher and has been working on the EDMS project funded by GE, for the past two years under my supervision.

Having worked with Dr. Yong since June 2006, I am impressed with his excellent research and development capabilities. He has a strong background in mathematics, image processing, and information technology. He also has deep knowledge associated with electronic document management. 

At GE Power Systems and other GE manufacturer centers, project documents for federal government and companies in United States and in other countries need to be carefully managed. Control of design documents through a product’s life cycle is also required for regulatory compliance. In traditional engineering environments, manual document handling and workflow process were used, which may lead to loss of data and time. 

With Dr. Yong's outstanding research and development in EDMS, it is easy to access, store, and track many types of documents. Also, electronic forms may be created to ensure that all information is documented while performing a procedure. Currently, critical documents and engineering drawings exchanged between GE and its customers, including U.S. federal government agencies, are managed by EDMS.

The research and implementation of EDMS also helps U.S. companies keep a competitive advantage in the international market by achieving enterprise-wide business and mission critical information management. Previously, an obsolete  program was used to manage Vendor Document (VENDOC) at GE Power Systems for all documents and drawings exchanged between GE and its vendors. With the outdated architectures, this system could not help GE maintain technical competitiveness over its major foreign competitors, such as ABB and Siemems. Recently, a newly designed VENDOC process in the EDMS, with secure document revision control and worldwide document access ability, has replaced the old system. As a principal researcher, Dr. Yong demonstrated his extraordinary research ability by integrating new techniques, and he is currently one of the top experts in this field internationally.

Dr. Yong’s current EDMS research project can also benefit U.S. government agencies. For example, Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA) is an U.S. federal government agency. For U.S. government projects, more regulation requirements need to be met for the recent two TVA projects: TVA-NEW01 and TVA-GALLATIN. For these projects, GE assumes the entire scope of the power plant design from the feasibility study to commercialization. The traditional manual Action Item processing method is not a acceptable solution for projects like TVA. By applying EDMS in TVA projects, documents exchanged between TVA and GE has been handled by EDMS; quicker response to TVA’s Action Items has be performed; and government’s regulatory and ISO-9002 standard have been met.

From GE’s annual report, GE’s revenues and earnings have reached a double-digit growth in 2008. GE Power System relies on the international market for its growth, and has brought more and more jobs to our nation since 2006. Dr. Yong is a principal researcher in the EDMS project at GE, who has effectively solved difficult theoretical problems and challenging practical problems. After published eleven research papers in international prestigious journals, such as Journal of Power Engineering, Information Weekly, and Journal of Document Management,  Dr. Yong has established himself as an outstanding researcher in the field of EDMS and information technology over the past few years. I firmly believe that he will continue to be a successful researcher in these newly established areas.

I hope this letter will fully support Dr. Yong’s petition in EB1 Extraordinary Ability category, so he can continue his research and development projects in EDMS and other related information technology fields. If I can be of further assistance in support of his immigration application, please feel free to contact me.

Sincerely yours

 

Alexandre Rosa
Senior Project Leader, Information Management
GE R&D Center
