RE: 1-140 form petition of EB1 Extraordinary Ability for Dr. Jassu Gupta

 

Dear USCIS officer:

This letter is written in support of Dr. Jassu Gupta's Form I-140 petition for EB1 Extraordinary Ability.

I came to know Dr. Gupta and his extraordinary research ability in my capacity as a director of the Sensors and Electron Devices Directorate, U.S. Army Research Laboratory (ARL). Dr. Gupta is an Assistant Professor in the Electrical Engineering Department at the Stanford University, and he has been working as a Research Associate in the Electro-Optics/Znfrared (EO/IR) Image Processing Branch of the Signal and Image Processing Division at ARL since January 2006. His research is focused on the development of image and video compression algorithms for military applications such as digitization of the battlefield.

The Army Research Laboratory's Sensors and Electron Devices Directorate (SEDD) is the principal Army organization for research and exploratory development of electron device and sensor technologies for Army battlefield applications. For the acquisition of digitized information, such as the location and identification of military targets from a battle-space through limited bandwidth channels, effective image and video compression techniques are essential. These techniques are used to compress the battlefield imagery, such as Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) and Forward Looking Infrared (FLIR) imagery that contain targets. Developing efficient image and video compression techniques is also related to the performance of the Automatic Target Recognition (ATR) technique that can actually locate and identify the targets.

A sad lesson learned by our armed forces during the Operation Desert Storm is the inability to distinguish between the friendly and hostile vehicles or equipment under poor visibility conditions. Consequently, more than half of the American casualties were inflicted by the so called "friendly fires". Certainly, we should not let the same tragedy to occur again, To overcome similar problems in the future, we need Automatic Target Recognition (ATR) systems that are effective and efficient in distinguishing enemy and friendly targets under adverse environmental conditions. Currently, a number of ATR projects related to different military applications are funded by the U.S. Army and the other branches. In the long run, these efforts could significantly contribute to the safety of our troop and the security of our nation.

Dr. Gupta performed excellent research work on the Face Recognition Technology (FERET) program which was sponsored by the Department of Defense Counter-drug Technology Development Program Office, with ARL serving as the technical agent. The primary mission of the FERET program was to develop automatic face recognition capabilities that can be employed to assist security, intelligence, and law enforcement personnel in the performance of their duties. 

In 2006, the major task of the FERET program, a government-monitored testing and evaluation of face recognition algorithms using standardized database and test procedures, was successfully performed. The availability of the FERET database and evaluation methodology has made a significant difference in the progress of developing accurate face-recognition algorithms.

Dr. Gupta demonstrated his extraordinary research ability on the development of the FERET program in several areas: the active development of algorithms, the availability of a large database of facial images, and a method for evaluating the performance of face recognition algorithms. With his knowledge and dedication, Dr. Gupta has cultivated a number of successes in the FERET program. He has designed novel evaluation algorithms and has developed several innovative face recognition classifiers by integrating techniques, such as vector quantization (VQ) and principal component analysis (PCA). Recently, his successful experiments have shown exciting results in terms of detection and classification rates, and some of his research results were reported in several international journals in this field.

In order to improve the accuracy and efficiency of face recognition classifiers, Dr. Gupta designed various projection-based techniques. His face recognition classifiers have proved to work very well on a large database of facial images and to achieve a stabilized classification capability, which was highly prized by academic experts.

Working on the FERET project, Dr. Gupta has made two original and very important contributions in the field. The first contribution is a creative design of the FERET database and evaluation methodology, thereby moving face recognition technology entered into a new era. The second contribution is that the FERET test has convinced the biometric community that face recognition is a key area of scientific endeavor. Dr. Gupta conducts the experiments thoroughly and produces appropriate results in a timely manner. He has assisted other research efforts in the EO/IR Image Processing Branch by giving insightful suggestions.

The ATR projects are of great importance to U.S. Army due to their versatility in many military activities. An effective ATR system that converts battlefield data into information for subsequent action is a critical aid for our soldiers. The Army and other services have allocated sufficient resources for the development of ATR systems to maintain the technological advantages of our military forces. 

I truly believe that Dr. Gupta has extraordinary research ability, and his research efforts will benefit the security, strength, and prosperity of our nation. Please approval his I-140 immigration petition based on his academic achievement.

Sincerely yours,

 

 

Scott Ford, Ph.D
Director of the Sensors and Electron Devices Directorate
U.S. Army Research Laboratory
