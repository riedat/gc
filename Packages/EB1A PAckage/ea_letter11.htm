Re: Dr. Praven Gurpur – Application for Permanent Residency

Dear Sir or Madam:

I am pleased to write this support letter, in favor of Dr. Praven Gurpur’s application for permanent residency in the United States, in the EB1-Extraordinary Ability category.

I am currently Professor of Pharmaceutical Sciences and Assistant Dean of Graduate College of Biomedical Sciences at the Western University of Health Sciences, Pomona CA. I obtained my PhD in Pharmacy from the University of Alberta, Canada. My expertise is in the area of liposomal drug delivery and solid dosage forms. Having obtained research and development grants from the federal agencies as well as 10 pharmacy companies, I continue to be instrumental in the formulation and development of several products on the market. I am also active in teaching pharmaceutics, pre-formulation, product development and novel drug delivery systems at Master’s and PharmD levels. I have published more than 60 research papers, presented more than 100 papers and hold four U.S. patents. I have co-edited the book – Liposome Drug Delivery. Some areas of my research interest include site-specific drug delivery using liposomes and monoclonal antibodies; controlled release formulations based on long circulating liposomes and polymeric delivery systems; and enhancement of bioavailability using lipid based delivery systems and evaluation in Caco-2 cell model.

I am one of the Advisors and the Principal Investigator of collaborative clinical research projects, with en-N-tech / N-terminus Research Laboratory in Pomona, CA. The projects are focused on the development of specialized dietary supplements called ‘bio-replenishments’. Dr. Gurpur is a Lead Scientist at en-N-tech / N-terminus who has recently conducted clinical safety and efficacy studies of a bone health bioreplenishment in post-menopausal women.

Dr. Gurpur earned his doctorate (PhD) in bio-physical chemistry from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) in New Delhi, India. Dr. Gurpur has extensive research experience in the areas of thermodynamics of bio-molecular interactions, drug development and molecular self-assembly, through his assignments at the Indian Institute of Science, Beckman Research Institute at the City of Hope National Medical Center, Baylor University and Louisiana Tech University in the USA. Dr. Gurpur is currently a Senior Research Scientist at en-N-tech / N-terminus Research Laboratory, a research-based biotech company located in southern California. He has published 15 high quality articles in international, high-impact journals and authored 3 book chapters. He has 3 patents and presented his work in several national and international conferences, and he is really at the top of the field.

Dr. Gurpur is highly recognized in the field, and has recently been involved with the development of a specialized bone health supplement (bio-replenishment) called ‘OsteoDenx®, at en-N-tech / N-terminus Research Laboratories. OsteoDenx® is a safe and natural supplement made from milk proteins, Lactoferrin – a multi-functional protein with anti-inflammatory and immune-related functions, and Angiogenin – a ribonuclease which promotes the formation of new capillaries. This study evaluated the effect of OsteoDenx® and calcium supplementation on bone turnover markers in post-menopausal women. Elderly women are known to experience bone health issues due to reduced estrogen levels after menopause. Estrogen deficiency leads to elevated bone resorption rates and increased risk for fractures. Dr. Gurpur's outstanding research has many direct applications for the health of American people.

Available therapeutic options for this condition, such as hormone replacements therapy etc. are prone to serious side effects. Dr. Gurpur’s clinical study, for the first time, clearly demonstrated supplement-induced reduction in bone resorption as well as enhancement of bone formation, as measured by well-established serum bio-markers. A significant improvement of bone turnover was observed without any side effects, compared to a control group of women who received only calcium supplementation. This pioneering work on ribonuclease-enriched lactoferrin supplement is published in Osteoporosis International, the official journal of the International Osteoporosis Foundation (IOF) and a highly cited journal that publishes topical and critical work in the field of endocrinology and metabolism (ranks at # 8 out of 93 journals in that field).

Dr. Gurpur has continued his work on this clinical study and recently has shown that ribonuclease-enriched lactoferrin supplement improved the inflammatory status of the treated women compared to those of the calcium supplemented group. Based on a study of several inflammatory cytokines and markers of inflammation such as C-reactive protein (CRP), he concluded that the immuno-modulating ability of lactoferrin accounts for this beneficial effect of the supplement. I understand that these noteworthy results will soon be published in Inflammation Research, an international, peer-reviewed journal of the European Histamine Research Society.

In the US, population of the elderly (aged 50 years and above) is predicted to increase by 60% by 2035, compared to base year 2012. This will likely lead to an increasingly frail population at greater risk of falls and fracture. By 2035, osteoporotic fractures and treatment costs are projected to grow by 48%, rising to an estimated 3 million fractures that would incur upwards of $25 billion in treatment costs. In this scenario, Dr. Gurpur’s excellent research and development of safe and natural bone health supplements represents a major step in the right direction. It is especially important due to the rising cost of healthcare and significant side effects of current hormone and related therapies. I do not have any reservations in recommending Dr. Praven Gurpur for permanent residency in the US, to continue his exceptional research on bio-replenishments for bone health which is in U.S. national interest.

Sincerely,
