Sample RFE Case Study: Detailed Analysis of a
Successful Approved EB1-EA Petition after Replying the Request For Evidence

1. File an EB1-EA Petition as a Postdoctoral Research Fellow 

Dr. Demidov contacted our Service shortly after he heard a successful story from one of his friend by using our Completely Do-It-Yourself EB1-EA package. He was also interested in applying for a Green Card under the category of EB1-EA - Aliens with Extraordinary Ability, and he wondered whether he was qualified.

Dr. Demidov received master and doctoral degrees in physics and engineering disciplines. When he emailed our Service, he was a postdoctoral research fellow at a top U.S. university. During his graduate and post-graduate studies, he had produced some original findings in a cutting-edge area of electrical engineering with commercial and military applications. Although Dr. Demidov had only six journal publications, they were being steadily cited by other researchers in his field. Dr. Demidov also had three conference presentations and one patent, as well as limited experience as a peer reviewer. 

We responded to Dr. Demidov’s email for his question and concerns about the EB1-EA application, and told Dr. Demidov that his EB1-EA application could be successful if he could get several strong letters of recommendation. Dr. Demidov agreed and purchased the Complete Do-It-Yourself package of EB1-EA petition from us, and he soon started to prepare his case.

Thereafter, Dr. Demidov and his recommenders worked together to prepare his recommendation letters, by following the sample reference letters in our EB1-EA package. He made sure that the letters should closely address the requirements for EB1-EA petitions. In particular, the recommendation letters should describe the significance of his research achievements, which would not be immediately apparent to the USCIS adjudicator reviewing his case, since he was working on a cutting-edge area of electrical engineering. 

Once the recommendation letters were done, Dr. Demidov worked to compile the rest of his supporting evidence, and drafted his petition cover letter by following the style and format of the sample cover letter in the EB1-EA package. In the petition cover letter, Dr. Demidov demonstrated his extraordinary abilities through his publications in prestigious journals, his presentations in distinguished conferences, his work as a reviewer in his field, and his merit-based membership in professional associations. 

After reviewing his petition cover letter, we provided our effective petition strategies and professional suggestions. Afterward, Dr. Demidov modified his petition cover letter based on our suggestions, and filed his EB1-EA petition using USCIS’ Premium Processing Service, to take advantage of the USCIS' quick adjudication within 15 days. 

2. Prepare the RFE Response with the Help of our RFE package of EB1-EA Petition 

After 21 days of waiting, Dr. Demidov received a Request For Evidence, or RFE notice, from USCIS Texas Service Center. Specifically, the USCIS adjudicator asked for more information about the significance of various aspects of his work, including his original scientific contribution to the field, research accomplishments, further evidence of his memberships and service as a reviewer, and the evidence that he had truly risen to the very top of his field.

Then, Dr. Demidov ordered the RFE package of EB1-EA petition from us to help him to prepare his response to the RFE requests and questions, and prepared 4 independent recommendation letters to show that his qualifications met the EB1-EA criteria. 

Dr. Demidov maintained a positive attitude throughout this RFE process, and worked hard to collect as much evidence as he could for the RFE response. Once again, by following the sample reference letters in our RFE package of EB1-EA, which answer various specific requests and questions asked in the RFE notice, Dr. Demidov worked with his recommenders to prepare the recommendation letters. We also suggested him and his recommenders to ensure that the letters should explain the significance of his accomplishments—for example, by describing the extraordinary ways that his individual research accomplishment had contributed to his field as a whole. 

Dr. Demidov prepared a total of three independent recommendation letters for the RFE response. He also collected proof of his membership in a scientific honor society, which further demonstrated that he had extraordinary ability, and updated lists of his publications and citations. He also obtained further proof that he had served as a reviewer for two journals in his field through the editors of the journals. 

Based on his supplementary supporting materials, Dr. Demidov wrote a convincing cover letter for the RFE response, by following the style of our sample of RFE response cover letter in the RFE package of EB1-EA petition, and answered the RFE requests and questions one-by-one in detail. Dr. Demidov first reaffirmed his extraordinary abilities, then gave in-depth explanations of his breakthrough innovation in his field. In addition, he cited the independent recommenders’ evaluations of his contributions to the field. 

After one week of his RFE response package submission, Dr. Demidov received notice that his case had been approved. 

3. Lessons for Other Alien Applicants 

There are a few important lessons that we can learn from Dr. Demidov’s case. First, his case shows the importance of maintaining a positive attitude and an open mind during the EB1-EA application process. Even though Dr. Demidov was disappointed to receive an RFE after the initial submission, he worked hard to have a strong, detail, and comprehensive response. 

Second, Dr. Demidov’s case shows that receiving an RFE does not mean that a case is ended to denial, even when the alien applicant would not be able to respond to each individual request of the RFE at the beginning. For example, Dr. Demidov’s RFE asked for information about the original scientific contribution to the field, he answered this request by focusing on his publications, research accomplishments, memberships, and service as a reviewer; his RFE was still approved.
