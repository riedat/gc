Subject: Dr. Allan Wang's Form I-140 Immigrant Petition for Second Preference Priority Workers - EB2 National Interest Waiver

Dear Officer:

It is my pleasure to write this letter in support of Mr. Allan Wang’s petition for the National Interest Waiver (I-140 Form) to obtain permanent resident status in the United States, and also providing the explanations that his current research and development in the national interest of United States.

I have known Dr. Wang since March 2009, when he joined our Semiconductor Development Laboratory of General Materials R&D Center at Charlotte, North Carolina.  As Dr. Wang’ supervisor, I am a Senior Scientist and the director at the Laboratory, and specializing in semiconductor, optics, and photochemistry of metal. I am also the 2010 winner of the Edwin D. Smith medal of the Physics Society of America.

Dr. Wang received his PhD from Institute of Physics, Chinese Academy of Science in July 2007. Thereafter, he was awarded "Alexander von Humboldt Research Fellowship" which is for highly qualified scholars and scientists of all nationalities not resident in Germany, enabling them to undertake periods of research in Germany.

As a visiting researcher in our Laboratory, Dr. Wang’s research interests are in surface physics, photochemistry of metal, and semiconductor materials, which include characterization of metal; semiconductor nanoparticles; electron transfer reactions; heterogeneous catalysis; environmental treatment by photo catalysis, and nonlinear optical spectroscopy.

Dr. Wang have been working on several research projects funded by Department of Energy and National Science Foundation. At the same time, he also has been working with Prof. Kevin Bruno at Department of Material, Boston University for an advanced composite material research project, funded by U.S. Air Force.

Dr. Wang’s current area of research is in photochemistry of metal and its application in semiconductor materials, Dr. Wang is also the inventor of the new instruments for electron transfer reactions measurement and many other high technology instruments in current use in our Laboratory, on which other scientist’s new work is based. 

After joining General Materials R&D Center, Dr. Wang has published more than ten papers in major academic journals, two book chapters on semiconductor materials and nonlinear optical spectroscopy, one issued patent and two more pending. Also, Dr. Wang have taught at the University level and worked in industry before, and now bridge the academic and industrial worlds with collaborations through research projects.

Dr. Wang’s excellent skills in semiconductor, optics, and instrumentation are strongly needed in our research projects at General Materials R&D Center. He always wants to develop new technology and the other side of the field. As a group leader in several research projects, he is very good at researching new fields and developing innovative methods. I am a hands-off supervisor, so Dr. Wang has managed the projects almost entirely on his own. Last month, he has had one paper accepted by Nonlinear Optical Technology, and another will appear later this year in Journal of Electrical Engineering.

Dr. Wang’ demonstrated his outstanding ability in the research projects, such as “A Pulsed Laser Proteolysis Study” and “Photonic Efficiency and Quantum Yield of Formaldehyde Formation”, both supported by Department of Energy and National Science Foundation. Dr. Wang’s achievements play a critical role in these projects and research programs. Examples of the Dr. Wang’s contributions to the current research project include his publications, patents, and breakthrough achievements in his research results.

As a director in the Semiconductor Development Laboratory and Dr. Wang’s supervisor, I do feel responsibility that the United States needs to keep its technology development in the global competition, and we should not lose excellent researcher like Dr. Wang. The same is true for the efforts we have invested here at General Materials R&D Center. I really believe that Dr. Wang will continue bring his excellent skill to further success of our research projects, if he could get Form I-140 Immigrant Petition approval based on his research in U.S. National Interest. 

Dr. Wang’s leading and critical role is important and significant to our projects here at General Materials. His research work is also important to U.S. industry and to U.S. homeland defense. His combination of skills and ability in semiconductor, optics, and instrumentation are extremely hard to match.

Dr. Allan Wang is an accomplished researcher and I hope we can keep Dr. Wang's talent and expertise. Therefore, I ask the USCIS Vermont Service Center to review Dr. Wang's achievements and important scientific contributions to our research projects and U.S. National Interest. Thank you very much for your review and consideration. Please call me at (619) 893-xxxx for any questions and further information. 

Sincerely Yours,  

 

 

Robert P. Cohn, PhD
Director, Semiconductor Development Laboratory
General Materials R&D Center
