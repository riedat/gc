Re: I-140 Immigrant Petition in EB2 National Interest Waiver for Mr. Kevin Yong

Dear Officer:

It is my pleasure to write this letter in support of Mr. Kevin Yong’s petition for the National Interest Waiver (I-140) to obtain permanent resident status in the United States, and also providing the explanations that his current research and development work is in the national interest of United States.

I came to know Mr. Kevin Yong’s research work and his potential ability in my capacity as a Director of EDMS (Electronic Document Management System) project at GE Power System. Since June 2008, he has been working on document management automation research and development for applications in engineering, ISO-9002, and government regulatory compliance, as a principal investigator.

GE Power System is the world’s leader in power system research and design. GE Power Systems concurrently manages many national and international power engineering projects, which may be located in numerous foreign countries. The EDMS project at GE is an important research and exploratory development of automating business processes and managing critical information.

To meet the industry’s strategic and mission requirements, such as quick Action Items response and appropriate Vendor Document (VENDOC) management, effective new process development and algorithm research are essential. These techniques are used to achieve the goal of having immediate retrieval capability of document in electronic form directly to an end-user’s desktop while maintaining document security. Mr. Kevin Yong’s recent demonstration of VENDOC process in EDMS, having the approval of all evaluation experts coming from both GE and other organizations, proved his superior research abilities.

EDMS research and development is also very important to the American people and government. In 2005, based on Library of Congress’ National Digital Library Program, the Digital Library Federation (DLF) was established by "fifteen of the nation's largest research libraries and archives to create, maintain, expand, and preserve a distributed collection of digital materials of our nation’s history and culture accessible to schools, libraries, and American’s home everywhere." In this program, how to maintain and preserve digital documents and records securely and permanently is a challenging research area. Mr. Kevin Yong’s current research and development project in the field of automated document management will have direct and potential contribution to save our nation’s contemporary and historical information.

An example that U.S. government agencies can directly apply Mr. Kevin Yong’s present work is the TVA (Tennessee Valley Authority) projects at GE, which are design and construction of STAG combined-cycle power plants at New Johnsonville, TN, and Gallatin, TN. These two TVA projects are critical to continuous economical development in seven southeastern states of United Stated for the 21 century. For these TVA projects, Mr. Kevin Yong’s work made a significant contribution, including assigning and tracking TVA’s Action Items, providing a timely and complete Action Item response to TVA, and managing various types of TVA documents inside EDMS.

The research and development of EDMS will not only help U.S. companies apply new technologies in their business management and product manufacturing, but will also help them maintain competitiveness over its foreign competitors in the international market. As the world’s leader in power system engineering, GE Power System’s orders increased 120% last year, which means more jobs for American workers. The contribution of EDMS to GE’s management efficiency and productivity is widely acknowledged by both the GE leadership team and academic experts.

Mr. Kevin Yong’s research and development work in EDMS also enables GE Power Systems to keep on being competitive in the global market, while making their products and services better and faster. According to GE’s 2010 annual report, "GE Power Systems won $10.5 billion contracts, and power plant orders increase 120% for the year," driven by record demand for gas turbines and maintenance services in the international market. Since 2008, more than 1000 jobs in power system engineering has been brought to U.S. workers.

Mr. Kevin Yong earned his Master degree in Electrical Engineering from Brown University. Before coming to United States, he had six years industry experience as a research engineer in computer-aided design and simulation in China. He has published several papers in scientific journals and submitted research reports to project sponsors.

Mr. Kevin Yong is an accomplished researcher and developer whose skills and knowledge in EDMS and information technology is in the national interest of United States. The EDMS research and development is one of the key technological areas in which the U.S. must dominate in order to maintain the high-tech strength of the nation in the global market. His continued work in this field and other related research areas will provide more contributions and prosperity to our nation. I believe that this letter will serve to strengthen Mr. Kevin Yong’s case, and that his petition will be granted. If there are any questions, please feel free to contact me.

Sincerely

 

Jeremy Collins
Director of EDMS Project at GE
NovaSoft Systems, Inc.
